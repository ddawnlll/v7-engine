# Alpha Harvest -> V7-Lite -> Tensor AlphaForge Roadmap

> **Status:** strategic sequence `LOCKED`; economic thresholds and promotion evidence `HOLD`
> **Date:** 2026-07-13
> **Owner decision:** XGBoost AlphaForge is pushed to its practical frontier first, including leverage-aware research. Validated alphas are then monetized through a deterministic V7-Lite path. A tensor-native AlphaForge follows when it proves incremental economic value against the frozen XGBoost champion.
> **Authority:** This is a cross-domain execution roadmap. It does not override Simulation economic truth, AlphaForge discovery authority, V7 policy authority, or Runtime execution safety.

## 1. Executive Decision

The strategic sequence is:

1. **Alpha Harvest:** use intelligently budgeted brute-force compute to extract the practical maximum from AlphaForge XGBoost, producing a registry of cost-honest, independently validated and economically distinct alpha candidates.
2. **V7-Lite:** turn only validated candidates into deterministic RuleSpec-driven decisions; progress through replay, paper, shadow, and tiny-live stages; feed realized fills, costs, funding and outcomes back into research.
3. **Tensor AlphaForge:** build a shared temporal/cross-asset encoder, first as an embedding generator for the XGBoost champion and then with direct neural heads. Promote it only on demonstrated post-cost OOS uplift.

This sequence is not a claim that XGBoost is permanently superior. It is a capital-efficiency decision: exploit the fast, strong tabular baseline while building the evidence, data and revenue loop required for a larger neural system.

## 2. North-Star Objective

### 2.1 Business objective

Maximize **survivable long-run geometric capital growth** after:

- exchange fees;
- slippage and market impact;
- funding and borrow costs;
- rejected/partial fills;
- liquidation and margin constraints;
- turnover and capacity limits;
- drawdown and tail-risk constraints;
- research-selection bias.

Raw return, raw Sharpe, classification accuracy and isolated backtest net-R are diagnostics. None is the final objective alone.

### 2.2 Metric separation

The following ledgers must never be collapsed into one number:

| Ledger | Question | Primary measures | Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| Signal quality | Is there predictive information? | IC, RankIC, calibration, conditional expected R | AlphaForge |
| Unlevered economics | Does the trade have edge before sizing? | post-cost net-R/trade at base risk, trade count, tail loss | Simulation |
| Leveraged utility | Does a leverage tier improve growth without unacceptable ruin risk? | expected log growth, drawdown distribution, liquidation probability, margin usage | Simulation evidence; V7 policy decides |
| Portfolio economics | Do independent alphas improve the whole book? | portfolio net-R, covariance, turnover, capacity, tail concentration | V7/portfolio evaluation |
| Realized truth | Does execution match research? | realized net-R, fill/slippage error, funding drag, reject rate, latency | Runtime/realized |

**Locked principle:** leverage may amplify a positive edge but must not be allowed to manufacture one in reporting. Every candidate retains an unlevered/base-risk economic score alongside leverage-scenario results.

### 2.3 Working optimization objective

For a portfolio return `r_t(w, L)` generated by weights `w` and leverage policy `L`, research should rank frozen candidates primarily by a conservative estimate of:

```text
expected_log_growth = mean(log(1 + r_t_after_all_costs))
```

subject to hard constraints on liquidation probability, drawdown, concentration, turnover, capacity and data quality. The exact constraint values remain `HOLD` until calibrated from real replay/shadow evidence.

## 3. Non-Negotiable Guardrails

1. Simulation remains economic truth; no miner, model or agent may bypass its costs.
2. AlphaForge discovers; it never issues live trade commands.
3. V7 owns deterministic policy acceptance, portfolio suppression and leverage eligibility.
4. Runtime owns lifecycle, exchange interaction, circuit breakers and kill switches.
5. Backtest pass is never live promotion evidence.
6. Every research trial, including failed and manually inspired trials, enters an append-only trial ledger.
7. The untouched holdout is inaccessible to search agents until a thesis/model family is frozen.
8. A negative base-risk edge cannot be rescued by leverage.
9. Large reported uplift with inactive costs, missing lineage or reused holdouts is invalid evidence.
10. Numeric thresholds in this document marked `LOCK_CANDIDATE` are starting hypotheses, not locked truth.

## 4. Evidence Baseline on 2026-07-13

### 4.3 First blockers to remove

2. Prove dataset, fold, embargo and holdout lineage for every full run.
3. Reconcile duplicated/contradictory alpha registries into one canonical candidate ledger.
4. Separate rule count from independent economic alpha count using return-stream correlation and mechanism tags.
5. Convert V7-Lite from fragmented accelerator scripts into one contract-driven, testable path inside existing domain boundaries.

## 5. Phase A — Alpha Harvest: Exhaust XGBoost Intelligently

### 5.1 Mission

Find the largest possible set of **independent, cost-surviving alpha streams**, not the largest number of rules. Ten near-identical ATR filters count as one economic cluster, not ten alphas.

### 5.2 Search hierarchy

Search is hierarchical so expensive compute is spent only where cheaper tests show signal:

```text
economic thesis
  -> causal feature family
    -> symbol/timeframe/regime scope
      -> label/horizon/exit family
        -> XGBoost model and hyperparameters
          -> decision threshold and no-trade surface
            -> portfolio interaction
              -> leverage scenario
```

Search order matters. Hyperparameter brute force cannot rescue a broken label, contaminated feature or uneconomic holding horizon.

### 5.3 Alpha family backlog

| Family | Examples | Why it belongs in XGBoost harvest | Tensor relevance later |
|---|---|---|---|
| Price/location | residual momentum, pullback, breakout, distance-to-range, volatility-normalized returns | strong tabular interactions and missing-value tolerance | embeddings may learn path shape |
| Cross-sectional | rank momentum, relative strength, beta-neutral residuals, sector/cluster spread | pooling increases breadth; trees handle nonlinear ranks | cross-asset attention/encoder candidate |
| Volatility/liquidity | ATR state, compression/expansion, spread proxy, volume shock | natural regime/gating variables | sequence encoder may improve transition detection |
| Derivatives | funding, basis, open interest, liquidations, premium index | direct perpetual-market economics | multimodal temporal tensor candidate |
| Order flow | taker imbalance, signed volume, trade intensity, OFI proxies | engineered stationary summaries are tree-friendly | raw/sequence order-flow is a primary neural target |
| Cross-venue | price/flow divergence, lead-lag, funding dispersion | sparse nonlinear interactions | shared venue encoder candidate |
| Calendar/event | session, funding clock, listing age, market-open overlaps | cheap conditional gates | usually stays tabular unless sequence interaction wins |
| Regime conditional | trend/range/transition, volatility state, correlation state | explicit specialists and mixture gates | neural regime representation candidate |

Every family requires a mechanism statement, causal timestamp specification, expected decay horizon and explicit invalidation test before large search begins.

### 5.4 Compute strategy: intelligent brute force

#### Tier 0 — Static rejection

Reject before model fitting:

- timestamp or feature lineage failure;
- outcome columns in features;
- insufficient symbol/time coverage;
- inactive cost fields;
- impossible execution or unavailable data;
- duplicate trial hash;
- support below the pre-registered minimum.

#### Tier 1 — Cheap screening

- small symbol/time slice with a train-only feature fit;
- simple linear/tree baseline and null permutation;
- feature availability and missingness audit;
- rough turnover/cost feasibility;
- eliminate dominated configurations.

#### Tier 2 — Successive allocation

- allocate larger windows/folds only to survivors;
- use random/Bayesian search rather than uniform grids for continuous XGBoost parameters;
- apply successive halving to weak candidates;
- keep a fixed exploration budget for novel feature families so the controller does not exploit one early winner forever.

#### Tier 3 — Full nested evaluation

- outer purged walk-forward folds for unbiased model-family comparison;
- inner train-only optimization for features, hyperparameters, thresholds and calibration;
- embargo at least as long as the maximum label/position overlap;
- multiple-testing correction over the complete family of attempted trials;
- block/bootstrap confidence intervals that preserve serial dependence.

#### Tier 4 — Frozen holdout

- freeze thesis, features, model family, hyperparameters and threshold-generation procedure;
- open the untouched holdout once;
- any modification after viewing holdout results creates a new family and a new untouched holdout requirement.

### 5.5 Required trial ledger

Each run stores:

- immutable `run_id`, code commit and environment fingerprint;
- data manifest hashes, exchange, symbol universe and exact time intervals;
- feature/label/model/config hashes;
- parent thesis and search-family ID;
- total trials attempted in the family;
- fold definitions, purge, embargo and holdout identity;
- gross R, each cost component, net R and cost-stress deltas;
- unlevered and leverage-scenario results separately;
- per-symbol, regime, session and volatility-bucket contributions;
- return stream or trade IDs required for correlation analysis;
- DSR/PBO/FDR or family-wise correction outputs;
- promotion state and rejection reason.

No agent is allowed to report only the winning configuration.

### 5.6 Leverage-aware training design

Leverage research is a **scenario surface**, not a scalar multiplier applied after the backtest.

#### Base target family

For each action and horizon, preserve:

- gross R at base risk;
- fees, slippage and funding separately;
- base-risk net R;
- MFE/MAE and path-dependent exit reason;
- holding time and liquidity/capacity features.

#### Leverage scenario family

Simulation generates exchange-eligible, config-defined leverage tiers. For every tier it must recompute:

- notional-scaled fee and funding drag;
- slippage/impact under the larger order size;
- maintenance-margin path;
- liquidation event/probability;
- tail loss and drawdown distribution;
- expected log-growth contribution.

Illustrative leverage grids such as `1x/2x/3x/5x` are experiment inputs only, not locked production tiers.

#### Model outputs

AlphaForge may train independent heads/models for:

1. action probability (`LONG`, `SHORT`, `NO_TRADE`);
2. expected base-risk net R by action;
3. downside quantiles/tail loss;
4. leverage-tier utility or survival probability.

The V7 handoff contains evidence for permitted scenarios. V7 still decides whether a leverage tier is eligible; Runtime enforces the final hard cap.

#### Acceptance rule

A candidate must first demonstrate positive, cost-honest base-risk edge. Leverage is optimized only after the alpha and portfolio gates pass. This prevents the search from preferring fragile, high-volatility configurations merely because their best historical leverage happened to compound well.

### 5.7 Candidate validation funnel

| State | Required evidence | Failure result |
|---|---|---|
| `DISCOVERED` | causal thesis, reproducible run, positive discovery metric | reject/repair |
| `OOS_VALIDATED` | purged nested WFV, stable sign, sufficient effective observations | reject/watch |
| `COST_VALIDATED` | active fee/slippage/funding; baseline and stressed costs | reject/reprice |
| `HOLDOUT_VALIDATED` | frozen family passes one untouched holdout | reject/new family |
| `INDEPENDENCE_VALIDATED` | mechanism tag plus low residual P&L correlation to registry | merge cluster/reject |
| `V7_LITE_READY` | deterministic RuleSpec, complete lineage, calibrated decision surface | hold |
| `PAPER_VALIDATED` | live data and simulated fills within model-error bands | hold/retire |
| `SHADOW_VALIDATED` | live shadow decisions and exchange-realistic fill comparison | hold/retire |
| `LIVE_MICRO` | explicit owner/risk authorization and tiny capital | rollback/retire |
| `SCALED` | realized evidence supports bounded increase | de-scale/retire |

### 5.8 Initial statistical gate templates

The following are `LOCK_CANDIDATE`, not permanent numeric locks:

- at least six outer walk-forward folds for primary comparison;
- positive median OOS net-R and no catastrophic fold hidden by the mean;
- positive lower confidence bound after serial-dependence-aware resampling for promotion-quality claims;
- DSR probability above 0.95 after accounting for the complete trial family;
- PBO below 0.20 for the strategy family;
- active cost scenarios at baseline, 2x and 5x; 10x is diagnostic, not necessarily a pass requirement;
- no single symbol responsible for more than 35% of positive P&L and no two symbols for more than 55%; thresholds recalibrate by universe breadth;
- candidate correlation below 0.70 inside a mechanism cluster and a portfolio target below 0.50 where achievable;
- minimum trade/effective-sample count determined by power analysis and dependence, not an arbitrary global constant.

These gates deliberately favor reproducible economic value over headline backtest performance.

### 5.9 Alpha portfolio construction

The harvest result is an ensemble of return streams, not a bag of rule files.

1. Cluster alphas by economic mechanism and residual P&L correlation.
2. Remove dominated variants within each cluster.
3. Estimate covariance with shrinkage and stress correlation convergence during market shocks.
4. Allocate a risk budget across independent clusters, not equally across raw alpha count.
5. Penalize turnover and capacity explicitly.
6. Evaluate portfolio-level costs and simultaneous-fill interactions in Simulation.
7. Use bounded fractional-Kelly/log-growth sizing only after estimation shrinkage and hard drawdown/liquidation caps.

Full Kelly is not a default. Parameter uncertainty and regime change make unconstrained plug-in leverage too fragile for initial deployment.

### 5.10 Alpha Harvest stop condition

XGBoost is considered practically saturated when, over several pre-registered campaigns:

- marginal validated alpha yield per compute-hour trends toward zero;
- new winners mostly join existing correlation/mechanism clusters;
- holdout survival deteriorates despite rising trial count;
- new sequential data adds predictive information that engineered snapshots cannot retain;
- the frozen tensor lab produces repeatable incremental net economic value.

Saturation is evidence-based, not calendar-based.

## 6. Phase B — V7-Lite Deterministic Monetization Path

### 6.1 Mission

V7-Lite is the shortest safe path from a validated alpha package to realized evidence. It is **not** a second research engine, an LLM-driven config mutator, or a bypass around V7 gates.

Existing accelerator scripts, outcome caches, reports and gate evaluators are retained where valid, but consolidated into domain-owned production paths rather than a standalone cross-domain god module.

### 6.2 Ownership-preserving target shape

| Concern | Target location/owner |
|---|---|
| Alpha/RuleSpec and evidence package | AlphaForge/contracts |
| Economic replay and cost calculation | Simulation |
| Deterministic acceptance, portfolio and leverage eligibility | V7 |
| Scan lifecycle, exchange adapter, order state, kill switch | Runtime |
| Operator visibility | Interface through Runtime API |

### 6.3 Deterministic decision chain

```text
validated AlphaPackage
  -> schema + lineage validation
  -> market/data freshness gates
  -> alpha-specific deterministic RuleSpec
  -> calibration / expected-R gate
  -> cost and capacity gate
  -> portfolio conflict/correlation gate
  -> leverage eligibility and hard risk limits
  -> paper/shadow/tiny-live mode router
  -> append-only DecisionEvent
  -> fills, funding, costs and TradeOutcome
  -> realized-vs-simulated reconciliation
  -> AlphaForge feedback dataset
```

### 6.4 Minimum implementation slices

#### B0 — Reconciliation and parity

- choose one canonical V7-Lite gate report and mark older contradictory reports superseded;
- reconcile cached outcomes with Simulation truth;
- require field-level parity for exit, cost and net-R semantics;
- fix inactive cost stress before any revenue claim.

#### B1 — Contracted alpha package

- immutable RuleSpec and model/feature/data lineage;
- validity interval and retirement conditions;
- supported symbols, regimes, horizon and capacity envelope;
- expected-R/calibration surface;
- permitted leverage scenarios as evidence, not commands.

#### B2 — Deterministic gates

- no free-form runtime config mutation;
- typed gate registry with reason codes;
- immutable simulation cost version and risk ceiling per deployment;
- champion/challenger isolation;
- idempotent decision generation.

#### B3 — Paper and shadow

- live market input with zero external orders in shadow;
- exchange-realistic paper fills;
- modeled-versus-realized spread/slippage/funding reconciliation;
- latency, stale-data and reject-path tests;
- automatic deactivation when error bands are breached.

#### B4 — Tiny-live

- requires explicit human/risk authorization outside this roadmap;
- smallest operationally meaningful notional;
- deterministic daily, strategy, symbol and portfolio loss limits;
- kill-switch drills before capital is enabled;
- automatic rollback to shadow on data, execution or performance drift.

#### B5 — Bounded scaling

- scale by realized evidence, never by backtest rank;
- use pre-declared steps and cooldown periods;
- re-evaluate capacity, slippage, funding and correlation at each step;
- no research/TPU budget is booked until P&L is realized and reserve/risk policy permits withdrawal.

### 6.5 V7-Lite done criteria

V7-Lite is not “done” when it produces a signal. It is done for a candidate only when:

- replay matches Simulation truth;
- paper and shadow artifacts are reproducible;
- kill switches and rollback are tested;
- realized costs remain inside pre-registered error bands;
- all decisions and outcomes are append-only and traceable;
- tiny-live authorization can be granted without changing research code.

## 7. Phase C — Tensor AlphaForge

### 7.1 Why tensor models come after the harvest

Tree models are strong on medium-sized heterogeneous tabular data and provide fast iteration. Neural models become most compelling when the input contains reusable temporal, order-flow, cross-asset or multimodal structure. The transition therefore targets information XGBoost snapshots cannot efficiently preserve, rather than replacing trees for prestige.

### 7.2 Canonical first architecture

```text
causal tensor [batch, time, symbol/venue, feature]
  + masks, time deltas, availability and lineage
  -> shared causal temporal encoder (small TCN first)
      -> 32-64 dimensional embedding + existing features + validated alpha outputs
          -> XGBoost hybrid head (champion-compatible challenger)
      -> direct neural action / expected-R / downside heads
```

The first experiment compares three systems on identical data and folds:

1. frozen XGBoost champion;
2. shared encoder embeddings plus XGBoost;
3. shared encoder plus direct neural heads.

Later experiments may test split encoders, attention/transformers, separate heads, mixture-of-experts and ensembles. They are not first-version requirements.

### 7.3 Tensor contract

Every tensor sample carries:

- exact event timestamp and decision timestamp;
- causal lookback boundaries;
- symbol/venue/timeframe axes;
- feature availability masks;
- missingness and irregular time-delta channels;
- train-fold preprocessing artifact ID;
- label horizon and overlap interval;
- Simulation cost/profile version;
- raw-source/data-manifest lineage.

Global normalization or representation fitting across future folds is forbidden.

### 7.4 Neural training objectives

Use a multi-task surface rather than a single direction classifier:

- action/no-trade classification;
- conditional expected base-risk net R;
- downside quantiles or tail risk;
- auxiliary regime/volatility/order-flow reconstruction where it improves generalization;
- optional ranking loss across symbols;
- portfolio/log-growth objective only after stable supervised heads exist.

The network never directly controls hard risk limits.

### 7.5 Precomputed tensor lab

Before renting large accelerators:

1. build immutable fold-safe tensor shards;
2. benchmark loading, preprocessing, host/device transfer and training separately;
3. run small TCN/MLP/LSTM baselines on a representative subset;
4. cache train-fold embeddings and feed them into the frozen XGBoost pipeline;
5. measure incremental OOS net-R, DSR, calibration, latency and compute cost;
6. run label permutation, time reversal and feature ablation controls;
7. estimate scaling curves by sample count, sequence length and parameter count.

This lab answers whether tensor representation adds information before committing to a full tensor stack.

### 7.6 Tensor promotion gate

Tensor AlphaForge advances only if repeated, paired comparisons show:

- positive post-cost OOS uplift over frozen XGBoost on identical folds;
- improvement survives multiple-testing correction;
- uplift is not concentrated in one symbol, fold or regime;
- drawdown/tail risk does not worsen beyond policy limits;
- calibration and no-trade behavior remain usable;
- training and inference cost fit the business case;
- hybrid embeddings demonstrate value before the more operationally complex direct neural head is promoted.

No arbitrary percentage uplift is locked before the lab creates a variance estimate. The test must be statistically powered from the paired outcome distribution.

### 7.7 GPU/TPU decision

TPU is not assumed to be automatically cheaper or faster. The actual tensor shapes, framework, input pipeline and utilization determine the answer.

1. establish a single-GPU baseline;
2. measure examples/second, accelerator utilization, time-to-quality and total cost;
3. benchmark TPU only after the workload is stable enough to compile efficiently;
4. rent the platform with the lower cost per validated experiment, not the lower advertised hourly rate.

## 8. First 30-Day Execution Campaign

This schedule is an execution target, not evidence that live revenue will exist within 30 days.

### Days 1-3 — Economic truth and ledger

- fix `fee_r = 0.0` propagation and add non-zero cost-stress assertions;
- lock the canonical trial/alpha registry schema;
- hash all datasets, folds, holdouts and model configs;
- reconcile `full_001`, `full_002`, registry and V7-Lite reports;
- define config-driven leverage scenarios and liquidation-aware outputs.

**Exit:** no candidate can claim cost survival with an inactive cost model.

### Days 4-10 — Harvest campaign 1

- mine existing causal feature families across modes/symbol clusters;
- nested purged WFV and multiple-testing ledger enabled from the first trial;
- run leverage surfaces only on base-risk-positive candidates;
- cluster winners by mechanism and residual P&L correlation;
- freeze the first candidate families for untouched holdout.

**Exit:** ranked candidate registry with honest rejection reasons and independent-alpha count.

### Days 8-16 — V7-Lite consolidation in parallel

- wire canonical AlphaPackage -> Simulation -> V7 gate -> Runtime event path;
- unify replay/outcome cache parity tests;
- implement typed reason codes, immutable risk/cost config and append-only ledger;
- run paper/shadow dry paths with no exchange orders.

**Exit:** a validated candidate can run deterministically without research-code mutation.

### Days 11-20 — Harvest campaign 2 and portfolio assembly

- expand to derivatives, microstructure and cross-venue feature families;
- run untouched holdouts for frozen families;
- build alpha covariance/mechanism clusters;
- simulate the portfolio, capacity and leverage policy together;
- retire duplicates and fragile winners.

**Exit:** V7-Lite-ready packages or a documented negative result explaining the remaining edge gap.

### Days 17-25 — Paper/shadow evidence

- run live market input through paper/shadow;
- compare expected and observed spreads, slippage, funding and opportunity rate;
- validate circuit breakers, stale-data behavior and rollback;
- feed realized discrepancies back into Simulation calibration.

**Exit:** evidence for or against tiny-live authorization. This roadmap does not grant that authorization.

### Days 21-30 — Tensor precompute lab

- finalize tensor schema and fold-safe shard builder;
- run small shared-TCN embedding experiments;
- compare frozen XGBoost, hybrid XGBoost and direct head on identical folds;
- produce scaling and GPU/TPU cost curves;
- decide `CONTINUE_XGB`, `HYBRID_ADVANCE` or `TENSOR_ADVANCE` from evidence.

**Exit:** tensor investment decision based on measured marginal economic value.

## 9. Agent-Army Operating Model

Parallelism is useful only when evidence remains independent and merges are controlled.

### 9.1 Work cells

| Cell | Scope | Forbidden overlap |
|---|---|---|
| Data/lineage | sources, manifests, quality, fold-safe datasets | cannot score holdout |
| Simulation economics | fees, slippage, funding, liquidation, parity | cannot change alpha features to improve results |
| Alpha family miners | pre-registered feature/thesis families | cannot access untouched holdout |
| Validation cell | nested WFV, MHT, bootstrap, PBO/DSR, correlation | cannot tune winning configs after holdout |
| V7-Lite cell | deterministic contracts/gates/events | cannot weaken Simulation or risk gates |
| Tensor lab | fold-safe shards, encoder challengers, scaling | cannot replace frozen champion metrics |
| Integration auditor | boundaries, reproducibility, report reconciliation | read-only on candidate scoring |

### 9.2 Compute allocation

Initial allocation should favor the current revenue path:

- **60-70%:** Alpha Harvest search and validation;
- **20-30%:** V7-Lite consolidation, replay and paper/shadow infrastructure;
- **up to 10%:** tensor data contract and small precompute probes only.

After XGBoost saturation or a validated hybrid uplift, the allocation can shift toward tensor training. Percentages are operating guidance, not locked quotas.

### 9.3 Merge discipline

- one campaign owner controls the canonical manifest and holdout keys;
- each cell works from immutable task IDs;
- no result enters the alpha registry without reproduction command and hashes;
- conflicting metrics are resolved by raw outcomes and truth hierarchy, never by report recency alone;
- negative results remain first-class artifacts to prevent repeated search.

## 10. Decision Dashboard

| Decision | Advance when | Stop/rollback when |
|---|---|---|
| Continue XGBoost campaign | marginal validated independent alpha per compute remains positive | yield collapses, duplicates dominate, holdouts fail |
| Send alpha to V7-Lite | base-risk, cost, holdout and independence gates pass | inactive costs, lineage gap, concentration or calibration failure |
| Permit leverage research | base-risk alpha is positive and stable | leverage is the only source of apparent profitability |
| Request tiny-live authorization | paper/shadow and kill-switch evidence pass | realized execution error breaches bounds |
| Scale capital | realized net-R and costs remain inside declared bands | drift, drawdown, capacity or correlation breach |
| Start hybrid tensor | fold-safe lab shows incremental information | no paired OOS uplift or compute economics fail |
| Start direct neural head | hybrid value is established and direct head adds more | calibration, tail risk, latency or stability worsens |
| Rent TPU | measured cost/time-to-quality beats GPU | low utilization or input pipeline dominates |

## 11. Research Basis

### 11.1 What the literature supports

- Large comparative asset-pricing work finds both trees and neural networks valuable because nonlinear interactions matter; this supports maintaining strong XGBoost and neural challengers rather than betting on a single family.
- Broad tabular benchmarks show tree models remain extremely competitive on medium-sized tabular datasets, supporting the XGBoost-first harvest.
- DeepLOB, universal price-formation and deep order-flow research show the strongest neural case when ordered, high-volume microstructure data contains reusable temporal/cross-asset structure.
- Deep Momentum Networks and Deep Hedging show how finance-specific objectives and frictions can be incorporated, but their reported results do not transfer automatically to this crypto system.
- PBO, DSR, White's Reality Check and multiple-factor research demonstrate why an agent army running many trials needs an immutable search ledger and corrected inference.

### 11.2 What public professional evidence supports

- Jane Street publicly states that neural models drive trading strategies and also says it uses different statistical/ML methods depending on the problem. This supports a hybrid, problem-dependent stack rather than “all neural” dogma.
- Man AHL reports trading ML components in multi-strategy portfolios since 2014 and emphasizes combining many weak information sources.
- BlackRock describes scientific testing, breadth, diversified signals, risk budgeting and portfolio construction as part of systematic investing.
- Citadel describes AI/ML, noisy-data modeling, simulation, rapid feedback and commercially impactful research, but does not disclose its proprietary production model stack.
- JPMorgan's public quantitative research discusses XGBoost among investment ML methods; this proves institutional awareness/use in research, not a claim about its current secret production stack.

### 11.3 Explicit limits

No public source can tell us the exact current production architecture, leverage policy or alpha of a proprietary trading firm. Their public material informs process design; V7 decisions must come from V7 evidence.

## 13. Final Strategic Rule

The program is aggressive in compute and conservative in truth:

```text
search broadly
record every trial
validate independently
price every cost
cluster economic duplicates
deploy deterministically
scale only on realized evidence
replace XGBoost only when tensor challengers prove incremental value
```

That is the shortest credible route to maximum return without allowing compute power to manufacture false certainty.
